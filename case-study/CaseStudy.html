Case study · The problem
01

When product identification breaks down at scale.

For any retailer managing thousands of SKUs across stores and brands, unidentified or mislabelled products are a daily operational reality. Each one costs time, accuracy, and trust.

01
No structured identification workflow
Store teams had no governed process for ambiguous, missing, or damaged labels. Every resolution depended on individual knowledge, manual lookups, and phone calls, with no record of how decisions were made.
02
Uncontrolled exception handling
When a match was uncertain, there was no escalation path. Cases were resolved by whoever was available. No evidence trail, no quality gate, no way to learn from patterns across incidents.
03
Downstream actions without a confidence signal
Fulfilment and labelling actions were triggered without an upstream confidence check. Errors surfaced only after the fact, compounding both the time cost and the reputational risk of acting on wrong product data.
Design intent Replace ad-hoc guesswork with an auditable, AI-assisted workflow that keeps a human in the loop for every uncertain decision.

Case study · Architecture
03

Four-layer architecture, capture to print.

Built to fit inside the client's enterprise environment. Cloud-hosted, EDW-integrated, print-broker compatible, and SSO authenticated.

1
Capture
Store associate photographs the item on Android. Evidence stored to cloud blob with signed-URL access controls.
On-device · cloud blob
2
Extract & match
AI extracts product features from the image. Backend queries the enterprise data warehouse and returns ranked candidates with confidence scores.
Vision model · EDW adapter
3
Review & resolve
Green auto-confirms. Yellow or red routes to the resolver queue, a web app where specialists claim, review, and recommend the correct SKU.
Confidence gate · resolver queue
4
Print & close
Confirmed SKU triggers the print broker. Full audit trail written: evidence, match, decision, recommendation, print outcome.
Print broker · audit log
Dependency-light design
Next.js + TypeScript resolver frontend · FastAPI backend · Postgres + Redis · Cloud blob for evidence · Async message bus · Enterprise SSO · EDW adapter for candidate lookup · Print broker for routing

Case study · Workflow
04

End to end. Incident opened to ticket printed.

A ten-state machine governs every transition. No step can be skipped. No evidence lost.

01
Incident opened
Associate starts a case and uploads product photos from the Android tablet.
02
Feature extraction
AI normalises image metadata and extracts attributes (colour, style, size, brand markers).
03
EDW candidate lookup
Enterprise data warehouse queried. Candidates returned and ranked by confidence score.
04
Confidence gate
Green auto-confirms. Yellow or red escalates to the resolver queue with all evidence attached.
05
Resolver review
Specialist claims the case, reviews evidence and candidates, submits a SKU recommendation.
06
Print & close
Confirmed SKU triggers the print broker. Audit trail closed with full event history.
